## ✅ Complete Verified Facts (Tier 1, defensible — publish-ready)

### Crisis Stats
- 40M people served by river
- $1.4T economic activity (James/ASU 2014)
- 7 basin states, Upper/Lower split (1922 Compact)

### Reservoirs (USBR April 2026)
- Lake Powell: ~3,526 ft, ~23% full, ~170 ft below full pool
- Lake Mead: ~1,055 ft, ~30% full, currently in Tier 1 Shortage
- Min power pool at Powell: 3,490 ft
- Lower Basin shortage trigger at Mead: 1,075 ft

### Negotiations
- Feb 14, 2026 deadline missed (KJZZ, PBS, NPR)
- Lower Basin 3.2M acre-feet proposal through 2028
- Federal alternative: up to 3M ac-ft/year cuts (~40% of 7.5M allotment) — Reuters May 15, 2026

### State Cut Pledges (Lower Basin)
- AZ: 760,000 ac-ft/yr — most cuts from CAP
- CA: 440,000 ac-ft/yr
- NV: 50,000 ac-ft/yr

### State Cut Pledges (Upper Basin)
- CO, UT, WY, NM: 0 ac-ft mandatory cuts, calling for mediation instead

### All 7 Negotiators (primary-sourced)
- AZ — Tom Buschatzke, Director AZ DWR
- CA — J.B. Hamby, Chair CA CO River Board
- NV — John Entsminger, GM SNWA
- CO — Becky Mitchell, UCRC Chair
- UT — Gene Shawcroft, UCRC
- WY — Brandon Gebhart, UCRC
- NM — Estevan Lopez, UCRC

### Quotes (all verbatim, on-record)

- Hamby: "Especially needed right now with how bad this winter has been." (NPR May 4, 2026)
- Entsminger: "The negotiations are extremely frustrating." (KJZZ Feb 13, 2026)
- Mitchell: "The time is now to solve the math problem..." (UCRC April 23, 2026)
- Shawcroft: "The hydrology is dire and it demands..." (UCRC April 23, 2026)

### Sector Breakdown (Richter et al. 2024)
- Agriculture: 52% (cattle feed alone: 32%)

- Municipal/Commercial: 18%
- Reservoir evaporation: 11%

### Nevada Conservation (SNWA primary)

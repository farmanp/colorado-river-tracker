## Response Protocol

### When you get a response:

1. **Log it immediately** in `outreach_log.md`:
   - Check "Response received" box
   - Add date
   - Copy full response into "Response summary" section

2. **Assess the response:**
   - **Factual corrections?** → Fix immediately, verify with sources
   - **Additional context?** → Consider adding to state card details
   - **Interview granted?** → Schedule ASAP
   - **Declined to comment?** → Note in log, perfectly publishable

3. **Take action:**
   - Update tracker HTML if corrections needed
   - Add "State response" section to card if substantial
   - Mark "Follow-up needed" if clarification required

4. **Acknowledge receipt:**
   ```
   Thank you for your response. I've noted [correction/context/position].
   [If interview:] I'll follow up shortly to schedule a time.
   I'll send you a link once the tracker is published.
   ```

### If no response after 10 days:

Send follow-up email (template in `outreach_emails.md`):
> Following up... planning to publish next week... last opportunity to review...

### If no response after 14 days:

**You're clear to publish.** Note in tracker methodology:
> [State] did not respond to requests for comment.

This is standard journalism practice. Silence after 14 days = publishable.

## Interview Protocol (if granted)

### Before the call:
- Review their state card thoroughly
- Prepare 5-7 specific questions
- Test recording equipment (get permission first)
- Have fact sheet ready with allocations, percentages

### During the call:
- Ask permission to record
- Confirm all factual details on record
- Ask open-ended: "What's the biggest misconception about [State]'s position?"
- Ask: "What should readers understand about the math problem?"
- Take detailed notes even if recording

### After the call:
- Transcribe key quotes immediately
- Update state card with new on-record statements
- Send thank-you email with publication timeline
- Log interview in outreach_log.md

## Email Best Practices

### Tone:
- Professional but conversational
- Respectful of their time
- Clear about publication intent
- No gotchas - you want accuracy, not conflict

### Subject lines:
- Clear and specific: "Request for comment: Colorado River tracker publication - [State]"
- Not clickbait, not vague

### Length:
- Short enough to read in 2-3 minutes
- Long enough to show you did research
- Specific facts > general questions

### Attachments:
- None (can overwhelm)
- Offer preview link instead if they want to see it

## Common Responses & How to Handle

### "We decline to comment"
**Your response:**
> Thank you for letting me know. I'll note that [State] declined to comment. I'll send you a link when published.

**In tracker:** Add footnote or methodology note. This is publishable.

---

### "That quote is out of context"
**Your response:**
> I apologize if the quote seems out of context. Here's the full paragraph from [SOURCE]. Is there additional context you'd like included?

**In tracker:** Add more context if valid, or keep as-is if quote is accurate.

---

### "Those numbers are wrong"
**Your response:**
> Thank you for catching that. Can you provide the correct figure and a source I can verify?

**In tracker:** Update immediately if they provide documentation. If they can't provide source, note the dispute.

---

### "Can we review the entire site before you publish?"
**Your response:**
> I've sent the specific sections about [State] for your review. The full site includes seven states and technical documentation. I'm happy to answer specific questions about [State]'s data.

**Why:** You're not giving editorial control. They can review THEIR information, not approve the entire project.

---

### "We want to write our own section"
**Your response:**
> I appreciate the offer, but the tracker maintains consistent formatting and sourcing standards. However, I'm happy to include a statement from [State] in a clearly marked "State Response" subsection.

**In tracker:** If substantial (100+ words), add expandable "State Response" section to their card.

---

### "Take this down immediately"
**Your response:**
> I haven't published yet - this is a pre-publication review. Can you specify which claims you believe are inaccurate? I'm committed to factual accuracy.

**Action:** Investigate their concerns. If they're right, fix it. If they're wrong, explain your sourcing.

## Red Flags

**Don't proceed with publication if:**
- [ ] Multiple agencies identify the same factual error
- [ ] You can't verify a challenged claim to Tier 1
- [ ] Major new information emerges that invalidates key sections
- [ ] Legal threat with legitimate basis (rare, but possible)

**In these cases:** Pause, fix, re-verify, re-contact if needed.

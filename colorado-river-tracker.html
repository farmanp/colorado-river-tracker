§ 03 — The Thirst Factor

Who actually drinks the river?

State cuts are political theater unless you understand who's consuming the water inside those states. The answer is uncomfortable for everyone: cattle-feed crops alone drink nearly a third of the entire Colorado River. Cities are loud — but agriculture holds the real water.

32%

Of the entire river goes to one thing: feeding cattle.

Irrigation for alfalfa, hay, and other cattle-feed crops is the single largest consumptive use of Colorado River water — more than all cities, industry, and tech combined. In the Upper Basin, 9 out of every 10 drops of irrigated water grows cattle feed.

Who has the lobbying muscle?

When the federal government imposes cuts, these are the groups that will fight loudest — and the ones that have the priority water rights in the legal pecking order.

§ 04 — The Accountability Gap

Pledges vs. what's needed.

The Lower Basin proposal promises 3.2 million acre-feet in cuts spread over three years (2026-2028). The federal alternative would cut up to 3 million acre-feet every single year — roughly 40% of the Lower Basin's 7.5M acre-foot allotment.
